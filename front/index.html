The gate

A castle of understanding: plain words in plain files, kept on this device. A text editor reads everything; the quill (castle.mjs) only writes what a hand could. If the quill broke tomorrow, nothing would be lost.

./castle.mjs                  the map
./castle.mjs save "…"         one line, saved — no questions asked
./castle.mjs loop             open a loop on a field with friction
./castle.mjs turn             turn a loop; spawn children; maybe close
./castle.mjs invite URL       bring one page in, labeled
./castle.mjs publish          render the public front
./castle.mjs warden …         autonomous turns (asleep until woken)
./castle.mjs help             the fuller list

The grounds: rooms/ hold insights (one true thing per file; quick saves land in the hall) · loops/ hold the work turning · keep/ holds what closed loops left behind · foundation/ holds the design, vows, manual, and warden · map.md is redrawn by the quill.

One rule has teeth: a loop closes only by leaving an understanding behind — reached or let go, the distillation is the one thing the quill refuses to skip. Nothing closes into nothing.

Three honest boundaries, all yours to open: invite fetches only the page you name; a woken warden sends the loop it is turning (with the vows and a keep excerpt) to Claude, every word signed; the front shows the castle's self-description plus only what you mark public: yes. Everything else stays home.

Built with joy, love, peace, and safety — kept as mechanics, not moods.

Begin in loops/open/ — the first loop is about the castle itself.
